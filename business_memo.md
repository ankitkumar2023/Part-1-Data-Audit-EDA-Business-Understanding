
# Business Memo: Customer Churn Investigation Priorities

## To: Product, Marketing, and Customer Success Teams

## Subject: Key Customer Churn Risk Patterns Identified During Exploratory Analysis

### Executive Summary

A distinctive audit and exploratory analysis have been conducted on all consumer profiles, buying behavior, assistance interactions, virtual engagement, and previous retention campaigns.

The goal was not to distribute reductions to every buyer at the moment, but to identify behavioral indicators related to increased churn risk.
---

## Priority Investigation Areas

### 1. Declining Purchase Engagement

Customers demonstrating lower order frequency and lower historical spending should be investigated for declining brand attachment.

Recommended actions:

* Analyze recent product relevance.
* Introduce personalized recommendations.
* Test non-discount engagement campaigns.

---

### 2. Reduced Digital Activity

Lower website sessions, fewer product views, and weaker campaign interaction indicate declining customer interest.

Recommended actions:

* Launch targeted product discovery campaigns.
* Improve personalized content.
* Identify customers with rapidly decreasing activity trends.

---

### 3. Negative Service Experience

Customers generating repeated support interactions, unresolved issues, or negative sentiment require proactive intervention.

Recommended actions:

* Reduce ticket resolution times.
* Prioritize dissatisfied customers for manual outreach.
* Identify recurring product or delivery problems.

---

### 4. Product Experience and Returns

Customers experiencing frequent returns, poor ratings, or delivery dissatisfaction represent a preventable churn segment.

Recommended actions:

* Investigate product quality concerns.
* Improve logistics performance.
* Monitor high-return customer journeys.

---

### 5. Acquisition Channel Performance Differences

Not all acquisition channels generate customers with equal long-term retention behavior.

Recommended actions:

* Compare acquisition channels using customer lifetime value.
* Reallocate marketing budgets toward higher-quality sources.
* Improve onboarding journeys for weaker-performing channels.

---

## Customer-Level Review Recommendation

The EDA notebook exports a list of high-risk customer examples for manual investigation.

The customer success team should review these cases individually before offering incentives because different customers may require different interventions.

---

## Strategic Recommendation

The company should move from a broad discount strategy toward a behavior-driven retention strategy.

Retention investments should prioritize customers showing early warning signs such as declining purchases, low digital engagement, unresolved service problems, and poor product experiences.

Future phases of this project will convert these business signals into customer segmentation and predictive churn models.
